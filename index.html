About Taganana

It is the most important town in the interior of the Anaga massif and one of the oldest towns on the island, having also been an independent municipality throughout the nineteenth century.
It has monuments such as the church of "Nuestra Señora de Las Nieves" or the hermitage of "Santa Catalina". Likewise, the church of Taganana has an important artistic heritage in works of sculpture, painting and goldsmithing. The place also maintains a rich architectural heritage in the form of traditional houses and stone presses.

Find more info in: Tripadvisor Wikiloc

The town also has a network of paths for the practice of hiking that connect with "Afur", "El Bailadero" and "Almáciga", as well as a self-guided route through the interior of the nucleus that makes known the ethnographic values of the place.

Some tips

  • At sunset it is convenient to close all doors and windows to avoid mosquitoes.
  • The water is drinkable but maybe a little bit hard taste due to the minerals
  • When you go out or at night it is convenient to close the parasols to prevent the wind taking them away
  • Please, do not throw food in the sinks, papers in the bathrooms and sand of the beach in the shower
  • If you hear any noise at night, do not worry, they are our friends shearwaters ;)
  • We do not have petrol stations in Anaga Rural Park, please check your fuel level before your arrival
